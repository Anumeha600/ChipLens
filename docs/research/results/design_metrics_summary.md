## 2. Property Generation Metrics (PG)

| Design | PG-1 Candidates | PG-2 Ranked | PG-3 Emitted | Types |
|--------|----------------|------------|-------------|-------|
| wb2axip skidbuffer | 10 | 10 | 10 | safety ×8, liveness ×1, assertion ×1 |
| PicoRV32 picorv32_regs | 2 | 2 | 2 | safety ×2 |
| SERV serv_alu | 1 | 1 | 1 | safety ×1 |
| Ibex ibex_register_file_ff | 2 | 2 | 2 | liveness ×1, safety ×1 |
| **Avg** | **3.75** | **3.75** | **3.75** | — |
| **Min** | 1 | 1 | 1 | — |
| **Max** | 10 | 10 | 10 | — |

**Key observations:**

1. **PG-2 = PG-1 for all designs.** Every property generated by `PropertyRunner` has a `confidence` key in metadata. The ranking stage does not currently filter or reorder.

2. **PG-3 = PG-2 for all designs.** No properties are disabled by default. All candidates are emitted.

3. **Property count scales with detected structural elements.** wb2axip has 10 properties because it has a detected reset (generating 5 reset properties) plus a detected handshake (generating additional properties). Designs without detected resets generate ≤2 properties.

4. **Property coverage ratio (PG-4):**  
   Each detected element with at least one property:
   - wb2axip: 5 registers + 1 reset + 1 handshake = 7 elements; 10 properties cover all → 100%
   - PicoRV32: 3 registers; 2 properties cover 2/3 detected elements → 67%
   - SERV: 6 registers; 1 property (module-level) → 17%
   - Ibex: 2 registers + 1 reset = 3 elements; 2 properties → 67%

   **SERV has the lowest property coverage ratio (17%)** — one module-level safety property for a 6-signal design is the most significant property generation gap in the corpus.

## 5. Diagnostics Metrics (DG)

| Design | DG-1 Issues | DG-2 Health | Severity | Confidence |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------|-----------|
| wb2axip skidbuffer | 2 | reduced | medium | high |
| PicoRV32 picorv32_regs | 2 | reduced | medium | medium |
| SERV serv_alu | 2 | reduced | medium | high |
| Ibex ibex_register_file_ff | 2 | reduced | medium | medium |
| **All designs** | **2** | **reduced** | **medium** | — |

**Critical observation:** All 4 designs produce identical diagnostic profiles (2 issues, reduced health, medium severity). One issue is always "Low property confidence (0.00)" — a systematic artifact of no-formal-verification baseline (see Note 1.1 in `baseline_productivity_results.md`). The second issue varies by coverage risk level.

**Issue breakdown:**

| Design | Issue 1 | Issue 2 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| wb2axip | Coverage moderate (medium) | Low property confidence (medium) |
| PicoRV32 | Low property confidence (medium) | Coverage low (low) |
| SERV | Coverage moderate (medium) | Low property confidence (medium) |
| Ibex | Low property confidence (medium) | Coverage low (low) |

The health label `reduced` maps to `medium` overall severity, which is produced whenever at least one `medium`-severity issue exists. Since "Low property confidence" is always medium-severity in a no-formal-run baseline, `reduced` health is universal across this evaluation set.

**Actionable finding:** The diagnostic framework needs a "no formal verification run" context flag to suppress the property confidence issue in baseline measurements. Without this, the diagnostics cannot distinguish between a weak verification setup (genuine problem) and a baseline measurement (expected state).
